Chandan Reddy

                        

Short Bio

Chandan Reddy is a Professor in the Department of Computer Science at Virginia Tech. He received his Ph.D. from Cornell University and M.S. from Michigan State University. His primary research interests are Machine Learning and Natural Language Processing with applications to Healthcare, Software, Transportation, and E-commerce. His research has been funded by NSF, NIH, DOE, DOT, and various industries. He has published over 175 peer-reviewed articles in leading conferences and journals. He received several awards for his research work including the Best Application Paper Award at ACM SIGKDD conference in 2010, Best Poster Award at IEEE VAST conference in 2014, Best Student Paper Award at IEEE ICDM conference in 2016, and was a finalist of the INFORMS Franz Edelman Award Competition in 2011. He is serving on the editorial boards of ACM TKDD, ACM TIST, and IEEE Big Data journals. He is a senior member of the IEEE and distinguished member of the ACM.

Research Interests

  • Algorithms: Deep Learning, Data Analytics, Natural Language Processing, and Web Search.
  • Applications: Healthcare, Cybersecurity, Software, Transportation, and E-Commerce.
